Transition Rooms

When you step into this changing environment, the space adapts its paths, navigating along with the visitors. As the boundary between the physical and the ethereal blurs, the work raises intriguing questions: are you guided through the space or are you involved in a joint choreography with it? This dynamic highlights the ubiquitous tension between human control and machine agency.

By building the Living Room with custom electronics and software that responds to motion sensors, Random International aims to create an environment that invites us to connect not only with the space, but also with our fellow visitors.

Amelia Winger-Bearskin is a multimedia artist and professor of AI and the arts. She uses artificial intelligence in her award-winning work to have a positive impact on communities and the environment. For her work she uses age-old stories that have been passed down for generations as a core for innovative forms full of animations and poetic texts.

Her work challenges us to see the world from new perspectives. In her project TO BODY, which can be seen in Realtime, she uses AI to erase all man-made structures from urban landscapes, exposing the flaws in our visible and invisible visual infrastructures. Through poetry and AI animation, she encourages us to explore new ways of perceiving, beyond the existing norms we have internalized.
